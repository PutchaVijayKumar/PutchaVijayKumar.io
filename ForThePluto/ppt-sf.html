Sustainable Finance - Meaning



Sustainable Finance generally refers to the process of taking due account of environment, social, and governance (ESG) considerations when making investment decisions in the financial sector, leading to increased long-term investments into sustainable economic activities and projects.



Essentially, Sustainable Finance focusses on three aspects


  1. Environment
  2. Social
  3. Governance

Sustainable Finance - Historical Perspective



Histocially, Sustainable Finance existed in forms such as Concessionary Finance, Grants or Aid and Mutual Assitance. It was restricted to certain sectors only such as Agriculture, Microfinance and Small Industry.

The below table shows the various terms that were used before the word sustainable finance became popular.

Terms Meaning
Philantrophy Finance / Venture Philantrophy Grants and long term start up grants plus other supporting finance
Charitable Asset Finance Mission and Program related finance
Development Finance Financing done by transnational development financial institutions
Ethical Finance Financing based upon moral judgements of performance
Social Impact Finance Finance that supports the social economy
Green Finance Financing that is focussed on climate change and pollution
Impact Finance That is focussed on measurable impact
